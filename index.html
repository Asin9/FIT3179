Top 100 Global Universities: 2023

The 2023 Times Higher Education World University Rankings evaluated 1,799 universities in 104 countries and regions. These rankings are based on 13 performance indicators assessing teaching, research, knowledge transfer, and international outlook.

Over 121 million citations from 15.5 million research publications and feedback from 40,000 scholars globally were analyzed for the 2023 rankings.

The following visualisation demonstrates this data, comparing various Universities results and how various factors impact said results. To find out more, check out the Times Higher Education website

Locations

The above map details where the Top 100 Universities reside, and their Ranking in the Global 100 through the colour gradient. This demonstrates that a large portion of the best universities in the world reside both in North America and Europe, whilst less reside in Oceania and Asia, and none from Africa or South America made it into the Top 100.

Through the annotations above we can see that University of Oxford is the highest ranked University in the World, as well as for Europe, Harvard University is ranked 2nd in the World, and 1st in North America, Tsinghua University ranks 16th in the World, and 1st for Asian Universities, and finally University of Melbourne ranks 1st in Australia and 34th in the World. The dominant presence of America in this list is even more evident while looking at the Bar Chart to the right,

which demonstrates that The United States easily outnumber every other country in terms of Universities in the Top 100, with Germany only having 9 Universities in the list while being Second in the List of Countries.

Students

The distribution of Males to Females within the Top 100 Universities is shown in the above Pie Chart, and it demonstrates there is slightly more Male Students on Average than Female Students, with 54.7% Males and 45.73% Females.

The dot plot below demonstrates the relationship between International Students and the University Overall Score, as it seems that the presence of more International Students boosts the score.

However, this is more likely just correlation, and not causation, as a more reputable and highly regarded University is more likely to draw International Students. Therefore, the amount of International Students per University is directly related to its Overall Score.

Teaching and Research

The chart to the right demonstrates a dot plot of Students Per Staff to Teaching Score. The Line Through the Graph is a regression line, to show the trend of the data, and demonstrates the relationship.

Asia and North America seem to follow the Global Regression Line closely, implying that more staff are needed per student in American and Asian Universities.

On the other hand, Oceania and Europe seem to follow the Global Regression Line only slightly, implying that staff per student in European and Australian universities are not as highly correlated in comparison to American and Asian Universities.

The Box Plot on the left demonstrates the Research Score Per Country of Universities. Through observation we can see that the country with the highest distribution of research score is The United States, with a minimum of 46 and a maximum of 99. The England has the highest Research Score Maximum, at 99.7.

Australia is the only Oceania Country to have Universities in the Top 100, and holds a median Research Score of 66.5, and a Maximum Research Score of 75.9. Multiple Asian Countries are on the chart, with Singapore having the highest Median Research Score of 86.45, and China having the highest Maximum Research Score of 97.4. Outliers are present in the countries of Australia, France, Germany, Hong Kong, and the Netherlands.

Industry and International Outlook

The Heatmap to the right shows the relationship between International Outlook Score and Industry Income Score in relation to Overall Score. There is a slight trend with International Outlook Score and Industry Income Score, as when Industry Income Score is around 70, 80 or 90, there are a higher number of darker blue squares.

The Relationship between Overall Score to International Outlook Score does not seem very clear based on this Heatmap, as the increase in Overall Score does not seem to impact the color of the square. Therefore it can be inferred that the difference in International Outlook Score for the Top 100 Universities is not directly related to the Overall University Score, and therefore the University Rank.

The above stacked area chart shows the amount of Universities Per Country in the Top 100 from 2017 to 2023. The area chart mostly depicts the number of Universities in United States and United Kingdom have trended downwards over recent years, whilst

the number of Chinese Universities in the Top 100 has increased greatly, from 2 to 7. Denmark, Finland, and Taiwan had universities in the Top 100, however in 2023 they are no longer in the Top 100.
