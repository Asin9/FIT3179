Top 100 Global Universities: 2023

The 2023 Times Higher Education World University Rankings evaluated 1,799 universities in 104 countries and regions. These rankings are based on 13 performance indicators assessing teaching, research, knowledge transfer, and international outlook.

Over 121 million citations from 15.5 million research publications and feedback from 40,000 scholars globally were analyzed for the 2023 rankings.

The following visualisation demonstrates this data, comparing various Universities results and how various factors impact said results.

Locations

The above map details where the Top 100 Universities reside, and their Ranking in the Global 100 through the colour gradient. This demonstrates that a large portion of the best universities in the world reside both in North America and Europe, whilst less reside in Oceania and Asia, and none from Africa or South America made it into the Top 100.

Thorugh the annotations above we can see that University of Oxford is the highest ranked University in the World, as well as for Europe, Harvard University is ranked 2nd in the World, and 1st in North America, Tsinghua University ranks 16th in the World, and 1st for Asian Universities, and finally University of Melbourne ranks 1st in Australia and 34th in the World.

The dominant presence of America in this list is even more evident whilst looking at the Bar

Chart to the right, which demonstrates that The United States easily outnumber every other country in terms of Universities in the Top 100, with Germany only having 9 Universities in the list whilst being Second in the List of Countries

Students

The distribution of Males to Females within the Top 100 Universities is shown in the above Pie Chart, and it demonstrates their is slightly more Male Students on Average than Female Students, with 54.7% Males and 45.73% Females.

The dot plot below demonstrates the relationship between International Students and the University Overall Score, as it seems that the presence of more International Students boosts the score.

However this is more likely just correlation, and not causation, as a more repuitable and highly regarded University is more likely to draw International Students. Therefore, the amount of International Students per University is directly related to its Overall Score.

Teaching and Research

The chart to the right demonstrates a dot plot of Students Per Staff to Teaching Score. The Line Through the Graph is a regression line, to show the trend of the data, and demonstrates the relationship.

Asia and North America seem to follow the Global Regression Line closely, implying that more staff are needed per student in American and Asian Universities.

On the other hand, Oceania and Europe seem to follow the Global Regression Line only slightly, implying that not as many staff are needed per student in European and Australian universities in comparison to American and Asian Universities.
